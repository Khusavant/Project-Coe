Holi: A Festival of Joy, Love, and Tradition

Celebrated across India and beyond, Holi has deep historical and mythological roots.

Origin of Holi

Holi is an ancient Hindu festival that dates back to the 4th century. It has been mentioned in religious texts such as the Puranas and even in old Sanskrit poetry.

Holi is associated with the story of Prahlad, a devotee of Lord Vishnu, and his tyrannical father, King Hiranyakashipu. The king wanted everyone to worship him instead of the gods, but Prahlad refused. Enraged, the king ordered his sister Holika, who was immune to fire, to sit in flames with Prahlad in her lap. However, due to divine intervention, Holika burned while Prahlad was unharmed. This event symbolizes the victory of good over evil.

Significance of Holi

Holi is not just about colors; it signifies unity, love, and the renewal of relationships. It also marks the arrival of spring and the end of winter.
